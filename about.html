About This Project

An interactive dashboard exploring the relationship between police enforcement and road safety outcomes across Australia

Project Overview

This interactive dashboard visualizes the relationship between police road safety enforcement (fines) and actual safety outcomes (fatalities) using comprehensive data from the Australian Road Safety Data Hub. Our goal is to provide insights into enforcement patterns, identify at-risk demographics, and evaluate the effectiveness of current road safety strategies.

Data Sources

We are collecting our data from the Australian Government's National Road Safety Data Hub, which is managed by the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE).

Enforcement Data

Source: Police Enforcement Dataset

File: police_enforcement_2024_fines.csv

Records: 12,179 total records (11,960 processed for 2020-2024)

Time Period: 2008-2024 (processed: 2020-2024)

Update Frequency: Annual

Comprehensive records of traffic fines issued across Australian states, including offence types, locations, demographics, and temporal patterns.

Visit Data Hub

Fatality Data

Source: Australian Road Deaths Database

File: Australian_Road_Deaths_Database_Fatalities.csv

Records: 57,852 total records (5,951 processed for 2020-2024)

Time Period: 1989-2025 (processed: 2020-2024)

Update Frequency: Monthly

Detailed records of road fatalities including crash types, road user categories, geographic distribution, and temporal trends.
