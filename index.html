Key Research Questions

01

Enforcement Patterns

Where and how do police enforce road safety? Analysis of fines by state, location, age group, and offence type reveals enforcement priorities.

02

Safety Outcomes

Who is most at risk on our roads? Examination of crash types, road user categories, and demographic patterns in fatal accidents.

03

Enforcement Effectiveness

Does increased enforcement correlate with reduced fatalities? Statistical analysis comparing enforcement intensity with safety outcomes.

What the Data Reveals

Speed Dominates Enforcement

Over 90% of all traffic fines are speed-related, making it the primary focus of police enforcement activities nationwide.

Age Group Patterns

The 40-64 age bracket receives the most fines, while younger drivers (17-25) show higher rates of mobile phone violations.

Single Vehicle Crashes

Single vehicle accidents account for the majority of road fatalities, with drivers and passengers being the most affected groups.

Upward Trend Concern

Road fatalities have shown an increasing trend from 2020 to 2024, despite high levels of enforcement activity.

Data Source Identification

We are collecting our data from the Australian Government's National Road Safety Data Hub, which is managed by the Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE).

File: police_enforcement_2024_fines.csv

Update Frequency: Annual
